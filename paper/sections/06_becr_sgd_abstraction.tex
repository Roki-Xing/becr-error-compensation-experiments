\section{\BECR-SGD Abstraction}

The $2$D repair theorem motivates a stochastic abstraction in which raw
discarded-gradient mass is transmitted through a bounded contractive operator
and only then scaled by a bounded adaptive recovery factor.

\begin{theorem}[\BECR-SGD nonconvex convergence]
\label{thm:becr-sgd}
Consider stochastic gradients $g_t$ with
$\E[g_t\mid x_t]=\grad f(x_t)$ and bounded variance $\sigma^2$, where $f$ is
$L$-smooth and lower bounded. Let $C_t$ be a sequence of projectors or
compressors satisfying an expected contractivity condition as in
Proposition~\ref{prop:becr-contractivity}, and let the raw-gradient residual
state evolve as
\[
A_t = g_t + E_t,\qquad
R_t = C_t(A_t),\qquad
H_t = A_t - R_t,
\]
\[
Z_t = \rho_t H_t,\qquad
Z_t^{\mathrm{eff}} = \tau_t Z_t,\qquad
E_{t+1}=H_t-Z_t^{\mathrm{eff}},
\]
with bounded transmission and bounded recovery scales,
\[
0<\rho_{\min}\le \rho_t \le \rho_{\max}\le 1,\qquad
0<s_{\min}\le s_t \le s_{\max}<\infty.
\]
Update the parameters by
\[
x_{t+1}=x_t-\eta_t\bigl(R_t+s_t Z_t^{\mathrm{eff}}\bigr).
\]
Then, for the SGD abstraction with $\eta_t=\Theta(T^{-1/2})$,
\[
\frac{1}{T}\sum_{t=0}^{T-1}\E\!\left[\norm{\grad f(x_t)}^2\right]
= O(T^{-1/2}),
\]
with constants depending on $L$, $\sigma^2$, the contractivity of $C_t$, and
the bounds on $\rho_t$ and $s_t$.
\end{theorem}

The scope of Theorem~\ref{thm:becr-sgd} is intentionally narrow. It is an
SGD-style abstraction of a raw-gradient residual transmission mechanism; it is
not an Adam convergence theorem, does not compensate compressed Adam optimizer
states, and does not subsume LDAdam-style generalized Error Feedback.
