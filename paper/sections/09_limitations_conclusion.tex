\section{Limitations}

This draft has several deliberate boundaries. First, we do not prove that
original \Fira generally fails, and we do not prove that exact \Fira generally
converges. The exact-Fira parity harness in this repository validates only a
read-only oracle on tested small fixtures. Second, Theorem~\ref{thm:becr-sgd}
is an SGD abstraction, not an Adam convergence theorem. Third, we do not
include neural diagnostics: CIFAR, MNIST, WikiText, and TinyGPT remain blocked
and are not used as evidence here.

Fourth, the memory accounting in this paper is synthetic and CPU only. The
artifact schema records residual bytes, projection bytes, and optimizer-state
bytes, but we do not validate GPU peak memory or claim memory-neutrality.
Fifth, LDAdam is treated as the closest related prior art, but we do not offer
an empirical LDAdam comparison in this draft. Finally, the corrected synthetic
evidence shows that clipping already repairs the fixed-stale orthogonal channel;
\BECR\ should therefore be read as a residualized repair mechanism, not as a
universal improvement over clipping.

\section{Conclusion}

The main message is narrow and testable: \Fira-style norm recovery is not Error
Feedback. In stale-subspace regimes, positive-$\epsilon$ recovery can send only
finite cumulative mass through the discarded branch and leave a nonzero
orthogonal gradient. Lower-bounded recovery and separated raw-gradient residual
transmission repair that failure in controlled diagnostics. The resulting
package supports a theory/mechanism paper centered on recovery-branch behavior,
while explicitly leaving neural validation, Adam convergence, and broader
optimizer comparisons for later work.
