By definition,
\[
\mathcal C_t^{\mathrm{BECR}}(v)=C_t(v)+\rho_t\bigl(v-C_t(v)\bigr).
\]
For the orthogonal projector $C_t(v)=P_tP_t^\top v$, we have
$C_t^2=C_t$ and $I-C_t$ is the orthogonal projector onto the complement of the
current subspace. Therefore
\[
v-\mathcal C_t^{\mathrm{BECR}}(v)
=
v-C_t(v)-\rho_t\bigl(v-C_t(v)\bigr)
=
(1-\rho_t)(I-C_t)v.
\]
Taking norms gives
\[
\norm{v-\mathcal C_t^{\mathrm{BECR}}(v)}^2
=
|1-\rho_t|^2 \norm{(I-C_t)v}^2
\le
|1-\rho_t|^2 \norm{v}^2.
\]
Since $\rho_t\in[\rho_{\min},\rho_{\max}]$, we have
\[
|1-\rho_t|^2
\le
\max\{|1-\rho_{\min}|,\;|1-\rho_{\max}|\}^2
=
1-\delta.
\]
Hence
\[
\norm{v-\mathcal C_t^{\mathrm{BECR}}(v)}^2
\le
(1-\delta)\norm{v}^2,
\]
which is exactly the claimed $\delta$-contractivity.

Finally, if $\rho_t=1$ then
\[
\mathcal C_t^{\mathrm{BECR}}(v)=C_t(v)+v-C_t(v)=v.
\]
So $\rho_t=1$ corresponds to identity transmission of the raw vector and
should not be interpreted as communication compression. This proves
Proposition~\ref{prop:becr-contractivity}.
