The $x$-recursion is unchanged from
Theorem~\ref{thm:positive-epsilon-failure}, so the same argument yields
geometric convergence $x_t\to 0$.

For the clipped recovery branch, define
\[
s_t=\operatorname{clip}\!\left(\phi(a x_t),s_{\min},s_{\max}\right),
\qquad
c_t=\eta b s_t.
\]
By construction,
\[
0<s_{\min}\le s_t\le s_{\max}<\frac{2}{\eta b},
\]
hence
\[
c_t\in[\eta b s_{\min},\,\eta b s_{\max}]\subset (0,2).
\]
The $y$-recursion is
\[
y_{t+1}=(1-c_t)y_t.
\]
Define
\[
q_y=\max_{c\in[\eta b s_{\min},\,\eta b s_{\max}]}|1-c|.
\]
Because the interval is compact and contained in $(0,2)$, we have $q_y<1$.
Therefore
\[
|y_{t+1}|\le q_y |y_t|,
\]
and by induction
\[
|y_t|\le q_y^t |y_0|.
\]
So $y_t\to 0$ geometrically.

Combining $x_t\to 0$ with $y_t\to 0$ gives
\[
\grad f(x_t,y_t)=(a x_t,b y_t)\to (0,0),
\]
and hence
\[
\norm{\grad f(x_t,y_t)}\to 0.
\]
This proves Theorem~\ref{thm:clipping-repair}.
